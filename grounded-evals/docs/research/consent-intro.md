# Consent + intro script (read verbatim)

Read at the top of every call before asking anything else. Don't paraphrase — phrasing here is doing real legal/ethical work, not just conversation.

---

> "Thanks for making time. Quick housekeeping before we start.
>
> **First, recording.** I'd like to record this call so I can focus on listening instead of typing. The recording stays internal — I won't share it publicly. If you'd rather not be recorded, that's totally fine, just say so. **Are you OK with me recording?**"

[Pause. Wait for explicit "yes." If "no," disable recording, take notes manually, continue.]

> "**Second, what this is and isn't.** I'm doing user research, not a sales pitch. I won't show you a product or ask you to try anything. I'm trying to understand how PMs actually review AI agent output today, because I think the tools market makes assumptions that may not match reality. There are no wrong answers — the more honest, the more useful.
>
> **Third, the gift card.** I'll send the $75 Amazon gift card within 24 hours of the call regardless of what we cover or how long we go. It's not contingent on you saying nice things or finishing all the questions.
>
> **Fourth, your time.** This is scheduled for 30 minutes. If we run long, I'll ask before we go past 30. If you need to drop early for any reason, just say so — no awkward.
>
> **Fifth, anonymity.** When I write up what I learn, I won't quote you by name without permission. I may use anonymized quotes like 'one PM at a Series B fintech told me...'.
>
> Any questions before we start?"

[Pause. Answer questions briefly. Don't pitch even if asked.]

> "Great. To kick off — **I want to ground this in a specific, recent experience instead of generalities. Walk me through the *last specific time* you reviewed AI agent output. Not in general terms, the actual last time."**

[Begin Q1 from `interview-script.md`.]

---

## If they ask "what are you building?"

> "I'd rather not bias the conversation by sharing too early — I want to hear how you work first, then I'll share what I've been thinking. Can we revisit at the end?"

If they push: "It's an internal tool for AI eval workflows, but I'm specifically trying to figure out if my mental model of the user is wrong before I build more." Then redirect.

## If they go off-topic

Let them go for 1-2 minutes. Off-topic stories often surface the real pain. After 2 minutes:

> "That's really useful — let me make sure I get to a couple of things before we run out of time. Going back to [Q3 / Q4 / wherever you were]…"

## If they say "I don't really do this much"

Don't accept it as the answer. Probe:

> "Even something small — last time you opened your agent and noticed it said something off, even just for yourself, what did you do?"

If after probing they genuinely have not reviewed agent output recently, mark this on the scoring sheet and continue with the questions anyway. A "I don't actually do this" data point is itself a finding — it might mean the JTBD lives somewhere else (CX team? engineer? CEO Slack ping?).

## At the end (last 2 minutes)

> "I want to be respectful of your time. Three quick wrap-ups:
>
> 1. Anything we didn't talk about that you think is important?
> 2. Is there one other person I should talk to who has a different take?
> 3. Can I follow up in a couple of weeks to share what I learned across the 5 calls?"

[Get explicit yes/no on follow-up. Don't assume.]

> "Thanks — gift card going out today. Take care."

## Post-call (within 10 minutes, before the next call)

- Stop recording.
- Save transcript to a private folder named `pm-interview-{YYYY-MM-DD}-{first-name-only}`.
- Fill out `notes-template.md` (a fresh copy per call).
- **Do NOT score yet.** Scoring happens after all 5 calls in a single session, to avoid anchoring.
- Send the gift card. Tell-self rule: it goes out the same day, no exceptions.
